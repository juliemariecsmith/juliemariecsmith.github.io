About

After teaching middle school and then homeschooling my own children, I decided to further expand my skills in curriculum development by earning a graduate degree and working in the field. I reluctantly agreed to work on a computer science curriculum project, making clear to the directors that I had no CS experience whatsoever (if we discount a few lessons in BASIC in elementary school).

I expected to muddle through, but what actually happened is that I fell in love.

I began learning to program on my own and then moved on to taking formal courses. I shifted my educational and professional goals from curriculum in general to computer science curriculum with a focus on equity.

Experience

May 2018 - Present

eDynamic Learning
Curriculum Developer

I created instructional content, labs, activities, and assessments for courses including Middle School Coding, High School Coding, Principles of Information Technology, Outlook, Digital Media Fundamentals I and II, Web Development I and II, Access, and Programming I and II. eDynamicLearning courses were recognized as a 2021 EdTech Award winner in Career Planning Solutions.

June 2020 - present

SageFox Consulting
Researcher, Organizer, and Coordinator

Under the auspices of SageFox, I have worked on two NSF-funded projects: CSEdGrad and Hindsight2020. The CSEdGrad project explored pathways for graduate students in computer science. After interviewing leaders in the field, I developed and managed community activities including study groups, a podcast, and a conference. I also conducted and published research related to the outcomes of these events. Hindsight 2020 analyzed the experience of providers of computer science professional development who had to quickly pivot to remote instruction during the pandemic. I was a part of the research team developing the survey, managing participants, analyzing the outcome data, and writing and publishing reports.
