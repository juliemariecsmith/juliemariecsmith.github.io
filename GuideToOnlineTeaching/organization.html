Organization







It can be difficult for instructors and students to stay organized when information might be spread across various pages of a learning management system, emails, a syllabus, assignment sheets, web conferencing software chats, and perhaps several other platforms as well.

Award-winning faculty online teaching practices: Course design, assessment and evaluation, and facilitation

Backward design is one of the factors associated with optimal teaching practice.

Using multimedia for e‐learning

When instructional materials adopt best practices for multimedia learning, student performance improves.

Implement backward design by starting with the end in mind. Begin by asking, "What are the learning goals for students in this course?" Then consider, "What assessments will show whether those goals have been met?" Only finally ask, "What learning activities will prepare students to perform well on those assessments?"

Become familiar with the Principles of Multimedia Learning in order to design and/or select learning materials that will be most helpful for students.

Present information in only one venue -- this is the key to avoiding contradictory directions, especially as you iterate over the course's design in the future. For example, avoid the urge to write "a 10-12 page paper" in the syllabus, the weekly overview page, the assignment description page, the rubric, and the assignment submission page; this duplication of information is likely to lead to problems in the future if you change the requirement in one place but not another.
