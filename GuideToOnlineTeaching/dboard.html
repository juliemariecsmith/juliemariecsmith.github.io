Discussion Boards







Perhaps the most distinctive element of online courses is discussion boards. However, students tend not to like discussion boards, although there are interventions that can change this.

Improving student attitudes toward discussion boards using a brief motivational intervention

Explaining the pedagogical value of discussion boards improved student attitudes toward their use.

Critical thinking in online discussion boards: Transforming an anomaly

Moderation approaches can affect the tone, content, and usefulness of discussion boards.

Research based practices for improving the effectiveness of asynchronous online discussion boards

Evidence suggests best practices for structuring, managing, and assessing discussion boards.

Actively participate in the discussion board, focusing on: (1) encouraging recalcitrant students to participate, (2) encouraging dominating students to solicit ideas from others, and (3) encouraging deeper thinking.

Structure the discussion board. For example, have a preliminary deadline for a first post and a later deadline for responses to peers.

Divide the students into smaller groups to encourage deeper discussion.

Create targeted, specific questions that require higher-order thinking skills.
