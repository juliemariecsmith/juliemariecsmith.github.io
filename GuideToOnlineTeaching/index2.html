Evidence-Based Best Practices for Online Teaching







a guide to online teaching

Studies show that college students in traditional, face-to-face classes usually outperform students who take online courses. This guide to online teaching presents research-based suggestions for instructors who are looking to improve the student learning experience in online courses. This guide is divided into four sections: presenceaddresses the issue of instructor presence and how best to cultivate it in online courses, communication addresses the distinct challenges of digital, asynchronous communication between instructor and students, organization provides insights into managing an online course, and discussion boards describes the best practices for student discussions.
