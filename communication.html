Communication







The importance of good communication between instructor and students is obvious, but it is much harder to accomplish in a setting without non-verbal communication cues of casual opportunities for questions and answers.

Student perception of helpfulness of facilitation strategies that enhance instructor presence, connectedness, engagement and learning in online courses

Students rate timely responses to questions and fast feedback as the most helpful behaviors of their online instructors.

Enhancing motivation in online courses with mobile communication tool support: A comparative study

Learning outcomes improve when instructors use mobile communication tools.

Accessible design is perhaps even more important for an online course than a traditional one; Designing for Accessibility, created by the UK Home Office, is an excellent overview of best practices.

Track the questions that you receive from students. Patterns might suggest areas where you could improve on the initial communication (e.g., a description of an assignment) in order to minimize the need for clarification questions.

Set aside time each work day to respond to student emails; delayed responses can make it difficult for students to complete their assignments on schedule.

Consider maintaining a document of common responses. For example, if you are regularly recreating a paragraph of feedback for students that explains a conceptual detail or a writing suggestion, collate those in one place so that you can cut-and-paste them as needed instead of recreating them.
