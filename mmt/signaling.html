The Signaling Principle

How It Works

You may be familiar with the selective attention test. If not, watch this video:

The point is simple: people will focus on what they are told to focus on. If they aren't told what to focus on, they will choose something -- and it might or might not be what the instructional designer wants them to focus on. To remedy this, we have the signaling principle, which states that cues should be used to focus the learner's attention on what is actually relevant.
