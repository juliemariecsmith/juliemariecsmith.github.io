Mayer's 12 Principles of Multimedia Learning

A TUTORIAL

Creating effective instructional materials is a challenge, especially when multimedia elements are involved. What features will actually help students learn? Which features might just distract them? Fortunately, there is extensive research in best practices for multimedia learning. Richard E. Mayer has developed and articulated these research-based practices. This tutorial website will introduce you to these twelve principles and equip you to design better instructional materials.

This interview with Richard E. Mayer is an excellent introduction to the principles of multimedia learning.

This tutorial will introduce you to all twelve principles. You'll learn how each one works, have a chance to dive a bit deeper, and then take a turn at practicing the principle. You can explore the principles in any order that you prefer.

Study these posters to learn how to design for accessibility before you begin to learn about the multimedia principles.
